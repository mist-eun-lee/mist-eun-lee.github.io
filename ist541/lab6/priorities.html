Managing Priorities

Managing stress effectively requires more than short-term relief. It involves shaping how we prioritize and act on daily tasks. The Eisenhower Matrix, a 2×2 framework of four priority zones, helps us distinguish between what is urgent and what is truly important.

Do Now
Urgent + Important
Critical deadlines, blockers, Tasks that need to be completed right away. 		Schedule
Not Urgent + Important
Planning, deep work, learning, long-term goals, and preventive measures.
Delegate
Urgent + Not Important
Status pings, quick admin. Interruptions, meetings, and tasks that can be delegated. They don’t really help you achieve your goals, but they do demand attention. 		Delete/Defer
Not Urgent + Not Important
Low-value busywork. Time wasting activities, distractions and tasks that don’t advance your well-being or achievement.
